5 Weird Animals

Aye-aye

Found in Madagascar the aye-aye is the world’s largest nocturnal primate. Often noted for it’s unique foraging techniques, it taps on trees until it finds larvae, gnaws a hole in the wood, and inserts its middle finger to pull them out. To find out more about this interesting animal visit Wikipedia

Saiga Antelope

A very unique looking antelope, the Saiga is known for its distinctive nose that protrudes down over its mouth. It has been known for its efficiency at reproducing in spite of human encroachment upon its habitat, although in recent years it has become more endangered. To find out more about this interesting animal visit Wikipedia

Axolotl

The axolotl is a species of mexican mole salamander whose larvae fail to undergo metamorphosis and thus the adults remained gilled and aquatic. They originated from the lake that underlies Mexico City and are often used in research due to their curious ability to regenerate themselves. To find out more about this interesting animal visit Wikipedia

Yeti Crab

More formally known as the Kiwa Hirsuta it was only discovered in 2005 deep within the South Pacific Ocean. Its hairy pincers contain bacteria that may be used to detoxify the water around the volcanic vents where it lives. To find out more about this interesting animal visit Wikipedia

Kangaroo Rat

With cheeks that extend all the way back to its shoulders it’s easy to see where this rodent gets its name. After filling them with food it empties them back at its burrow by turning them inside out with its paws. To find out more about this interesting animal visit Wikipedia
